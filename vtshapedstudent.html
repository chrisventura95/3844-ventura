VT-Shaped Essay

This rap song reflects the VT-shaped student model in various ways. The verses tell the story of an English student who possesses the knowledge to write HTML, use Atom, and use GitHub. With this knowledge, the student is confident he will succeed in life. By exploring the addition of technology curricula into his learning, as well as his internship experience, the rap song reflects the VT-shaped student model while offering a critique on past stereotypes of the liberal arts student.

The student looks back on his prior workload, which consisted of literature workshops. While he enjoyed his liberal curricula, the student acknowledges that a lot has changed since then: he has picked up coding lessons and implements them in his work. Through this combination of arts and technology, the student exemplifies the T-shaped concept. Additionally, the student has gained experience at his internship, where he now has hands-on experience that enhances his learning opportunities. This aligns with the V-shaped concept, and with all of this taken together, the student successfully possesses the abilities of a VT-shaped student.

In terms of critical design, the student questions the negative stereotypes linked to English students. Prior to learning about coding, the student was at a loss because he only possessed humanistic knowledge. Furthermore, peers would mock him for being an English major. Thanks to the implementation of the VT-shaped student, English majors are now instilled with confidence to live successful and wealthy lives as they create versatile content.

Thanks to the VT-shaped student the liberal arts department has gained a better reputation and better-prepared students. The implementation of useful technology has endowed students with the skillset needed to strive in our advancing society.
